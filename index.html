February 26, 2023

To: Parents
From: Mr. Pete and Ms. Josie / 1st Grade Faith Formation

On Sunday (Feb 19), we talked to the children about Lent. We explained that Lent starts on Ash Wednesday, lasts 40 days, and ends on Holy Thursday. During the season of Lent we are called to pray, give to people in need, and to try our best to bring the light of Jesus to others.

Next Sunday (March 5), we will begin collecting non-perishable food items for St. Vincent de Paul. Acceptable items include: canned meat, fish, vegetables, & soups; coffee & tea; boxed mac & cheese, etc. We're asking each child to bring 2 or 3 items. We hope to have this donation ready one week before the end of Lent.

Please read the following 'My Lent To Do List' for suggestions on how to help bring your child and family closer to God during this Season of Lent. We have also included a link to the 2023 Lenten Activity Calendar if you're interested in a Lent specific activity for your child.

Thank you, Pete and Josie Reynolds (210-363-0347). Choose Joy!

My Lent To Do List

Give to People In Need

Pray

  • Pray the Rosary with your family.
  • Make the Sign of The Cross.
  • Say the Apostle's Creed, the Our Father, the Hail Mary, & the Glory Be prayers.
  • Say Grace before Meals
  • Go to Mass on Sunday and go to special Lent services.
  • Visit the Adoration Chapel and spend time with Jesus.
  • TALK to JESUS and MOTHER MARY.

Do Good

  • Obey your parents and guardians.
  • Do NOT argue, answer back or give attitude.
  • Respect and Love your parents and guardians.

Show Love & Kindness

  • Be kind, loving, caring, and helpful to everyone.
  • Share your things with others, and remember to SMILE.
  • Visit your family members.
  • Remember JOY. Put Jesus and Others first. Put yourself last and BE THE LIGHT OF THE WORLD.
  • Show the HEART and the WORDS of Jesus.

Sacrifice

  • Watch less TV.
  • Spend less time playing video games, or even better, stop playing video games.
  • Give up your favorite candy, food or drink.
